# Known Verification Traps — Economics Careers Australia

Employer-specific gotchas. **Read this file before finalising any draft.**

---

## Role-type traps

- **The trap**: Employers use inconsistent labels. "Vacationer", "Vacation Program", "Summer Internship", "Summer Analyst", and "Internship" can refer to the same broad penultimate-year pathway. "Analyst" can mean a graduate-entry analyst role or an experienced-hire role.
- **Verification rule**: Use the employer's exact program title in `Program name`, then choose the closest spreadsheet `Type`. If an analyst job is open to graduates or early-career applicants, use `Analyst Job`; if it requires substantial experience, omit it.
- **Do not miss these keywords**: internship, intern, graduate program, graduate job, graduate economist, analyst, research analyst, policy analyst, cadetship, cadet, vacation program, vacationer, summer vacation, summer analyst, industry placement, scholarship.

---

## Treasury

- **The trap**: Treasury runs multiple distinct programs that look similar but aren't — the Graduate Program has Economics, Tax, and Markets streams; there's also a separate Summer Vacation Program (penultimate-year) and an Indigenous Economic Cadetship. Different deadlines, different applicant pools.
- **Verification rule**: Check the Treasury careers page for each program separately. Don't lump them into one row in the spreadsheet.

## Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA)

- **The trap**: Three distinct programs — Graduate Program (Economist and Analyst streams, final-year + honours), Cadetship Program (penultimate-year, part-time during study), and Summer Internship. Easy to conflate.
- **Verification rule**: Three rows in the spreadsheet, not one. The Economist stream requires Honours or equivalent — flag this.

## Productivity Commission

- **The trap**: The Graduate Development Program and Cadetship Program have offset application windows; the Summer Research Program is separate from both.
- **Verification rule**: Check the PC careers page for current intake. The PC is small so any given year may not have all three open.

## Macquarie Group

- **The trap**: Macquarie has four very different business groups (Macquarie Capital, Commodities & Global Markets, Banking & Financial Services, Macquarie Asset Management). Each runs its own Graduate Program and Vacation Program intake. Sometimes one group's applications open while another's are closed.
- **Verification rule**: The Macquarie careers page lists each separately. If listing Macquarie, identify which group is currently open.

## Big 4 Banks (CBA, ANZ, NAB, Westpac)

- **The trap**: Each bank runs many streams (Retail, Institutional, Markets, Technology, Risk, etc.) — economics-relevant streams are usually under Institutional or Markets. Generic "Graduate Program" applications may not surface economics roles.
- **Verification rule**: When verifying, look for the Institutional / Markets / Economics-specific stream. The newsletter should mention which stream(s) are economics-relevant.

## Frontier Economics

- **The trap**: Frontier Economics Australia is independent from Frontier Economics UK/Europe — different applications, different cycles.
- **Verification rule**: Use frontier-economics.com.au, not frontier-economics.com. Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane offices may hire on different cycles.

## Deloitte Access Economics

- **The trap**: Sits inside Deloitte's general graduate program. Applying to Deloitte broadly doesn't guarantee you land in DAE.
- **Verification rule**: Note in the description that applicants should specifically request the Deloitte Access Economics group in their preferences.

## State Treasuries

- **The trap**: State Treasury graduate programs often run alongside whole-of-government graduate programs (e.g. NSW Government Graduate Program is separate from NSW Treasury Graduate Program). Confusing for students.
- **Verification rule**: List the Treasury-specific program. If the whole-of-government one is also open and economics-relevant, list it separately with a clear distinction in the program name column.

## McKinsey / Bain / BCG

- **The trap**: Application windows are very strict, with multiple staged programs (Bridge programs, Insight days, Summer Internships, Full-time). Easy to list one program when the actual currently-open one is different.
- **Verification rule**: Check the firm's "students" or "campus" page directly. Don't trust LinkedIn job snippets — these firms rotate roles quickly.

## Big 4 (Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG) graduate intakes

- **The trap**: Most Big 4 grad programs are pan-service-line. The economics-relevant work happens in specific practices (Deloitte Access Economics, KPMG Economics, PwC Strategy&, EY Strategy and Transactions).
- **Verification rule**: Direct the audience to the specific practice when possible. Generic "Big 4 Grad Program" listings are low-information.

## Asset managers — penultimate vs final year

- **The trap**: Asset manager graduate programs are usually final-year; their internships are penultimate-year. But some (e.g. boutique PE firms) blur this.
- **Verification rule**: Check the individual program description for the year-of-study eligibility.

## "Coming soon" pages

- **The trap**: Some graduate program pages display "Applications open soon" or "Coming soon" without actually being open. Listing one of these as open is a false positive.
- **Verification rule**: The application must have a working link to a submission form OR a clearly-stated date that's in the future, not just "soon".

## Australian citizenship requirement

- **The trap**: Almost all federal government grad programs require Australian citizenship (some accept permanent residents). International students at Australian universities are often eligible for private-sector roles but locked out of public-sector ones.
- **Verification rule**: When the live page specifies "Australian citizen only" or "Australian citizen / PR", reflect that accurately in the Notes column and the description. This is a major filter for many students.

## Honours / Postgraduate requirement

- **The trap**: Some programs (RBA Economist, Treasury Economics stream, PBO, some IMF roles) explicitly require Honours or postgraduate qualifications. Listing these as available to "any student" is misleading.
- **Verification rule**: Flag any Honours / postgrad requirement in the Year of study column.

## Discontinued / merged programs

- **Pendal Group** — merged with Perpetual; verify whether the Pendal program still exists separately.
- **Bankwest** — operations consolidated into CBA; check whether a separate Bankwest grad program still exists.
- **AlphaBeta** — acquired by Accenture; now part of Accenture Strategy.

---

## General verification protocol

1. **Direct fetch** the specific program page or careers portal listing.
2. Confirm any listed deadline is after today.
3. Confirm the program is open to the typical audience (penultimate-year for vacation, final-year for grad). If it's only open to e.g. final-year MBA students, flag in Notes.
4. When uncertain, **omit the program** rather than risk listing a closed one.
5. The spreadsheet attachment follows the same rule — closed programs are excluded.
