## Program entries

Each program entry has three parts:

1. **Header line**: `Organisation — Program Name | Status or Deadline`
2. **Description paragraph**: 3–5 sentences covering what the role is, who it's for (penultimate-year / final-year / postgraduate), location, pay, and how to apply.
3. **URL line**: bare URL on its own line, no markdown link syntax.

Example:

```
Reserve Bank of Australia — 2027 Graduate Program | Deadline: 14 March 2026

The RBA's flagship Graduate Program for economists and analysts, based in Sydney. Two streams: Economist (research-focused, requires honours or equivalent) and Analyst (operations, markets, payments). Rotations across the bank in the first year, salary ~$95,000 plus generous super and study assistance. Open to Australian citizens and permanent residents only.

https://www.rba.gov.au/careers/graduate.html
```

## Ordering within sections

Within each sector section, order by:
1. **Type** — Graduate Programs and Graduate Jobs first, then Analyst Jobs, then Cadetships, then Internships/Vacationer programs, then Industry Placements, Scholarships, and Other
2. **Deadline** — sooner first, rolling/undated last
3. **Tier** — major employers first (Treasury before a small state department; CBA before Bendigo Bank)

## Formatting notes

- **No markdown asterisks or bold** in the body — Gmail's plain-text composer renders them as literal asterisks.
- **No emojis beyond the sector header emojis.**
- **Don't use the word "genuinely".**
- **No political framing.** This newsletter is sector-comprehensive and politically neutral. Avoid labels like "centre-right" or "left-leaning" in program descriptions — describe each employer factually by what they do.
- **Date format**: `31 May 2026` (day month year).
- **Currency**: AUD for all pay figures; spell out as e.g. "$95,000" or "$110,000+".

## Honest hedging language

When a deadline can't be verified from the live page:
- "Rolling" — for programs accepting rolling applications
- "Apply early" — when no deadline is shown but the program is dated
- "Annual cycle — check live page" — when the live page hasn't yet posted the next cycle's dates
- "Applications open" — when the page confirms applications are accepting but no deadline is given

Never invent a specific deadline.

## Australian-specific quirks worth noting in descriptions

- **Citizenship**: most federal government grad programs require Australian citizenship. State Treasury programs usually too. Mention when relevant.
- **Final-year vs penultimate-year**: Graduate Programs and Graduate Jobs are usually for students in their final year of study or recent graduates. Analyst Jobs may be graduate-entry or experienced-hire, so check carefully. Vacationer Programs, Summer Internships, Industry Placements, and Cadetships are typically for penultimate-year undergrads. Don't confuse them in descriptions.
- **Honours / postgrad requirement**: Some programs (RBA Economist stream, Treasury Economics) require Honours or a postgraduate degree. Mention this.
- **Security clearance**: Defence, DFAT, and some intelligence-adjacent agencies require security clearance. Mention this for transparency.
